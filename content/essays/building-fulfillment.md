## The Overarching Goal

Put simply, peace precedes fulfillment. These two attributes are strongly intertwined. The growth of one leads to growth of the other. In some sense, they are the same thing. I will outline several key principles that build peace and fulfillment.

## Mental Strength

It is helpful to think of your mind as a structure that you build and work towards throughout your life. Consider a grand mansion or castle befitting of spending one's lifetime building. If built correctly, this structure can withstand violent storms or attacks from the outside. Housed within your mind is your greatest treasure — peace. The structure that houses it requires intentional time and effort to build.

## Knowing Yourself

Let me start with the most important principle: The path towards fulfillment begins with self-understanding. As knowledge of yourself grows, so does your capacity for fulfillment.

Without a dedicated effort to understand your story, you cannot answer what it is that fulfills you. Without an answer you cannot make a conscious effort towards building a fulfilling life.

Therefore, think about your life and answer some questions for yourself.

- What were times in which you felt most fulfilled? What were the key factors?
- What is it that brings you energy?

For me, those answers converged on these three things.

1. Work towards something great
2. Relationships with people that matter
3. Health

## How to Construct

These pillars are built by actions repeated over long periods of time. The currency for action is energy. Without energy you cannot take actions towards fulfillment.

Be very conscious and selfish with your energy. This is a finite resources that renews daily. Stress inducers, social media, and people will constantly vie for your energy. Guard it closely and allocate as much as you can towards your pillars.

## Balancing the Pillars

Reaching the frontier in one field seems like great, meaningful work. Sticking to one thing is the hard part. We each have so many individual hobbies and projects that we resonate with. Here's the plan: Work on the things that are most interesting to you. As you find activities you're most aligned with, you will spend more and more energy on the most important ones. Crucially, the work must be continually engaging. This maintains your energy which allows you to make repeated actions towards strengthening your pillars.

Becoming great by reaching the frontier does not have to be a lonely path. It can be an incredible ride, you dictate this. It does not have to be all or nothing. Find patience and seek balance as you build. We are multi-faceted humans that are capable of working on multiple projects at once. Laws of neuroplasticity state that repeated actions over time will make a greater difference than a singular concerted push over several days. To work tirelessly towards a goal is unsustainable. It also does not lead to much change. Instead, daily action that incrementally compounds will sustainably lead towards success.
