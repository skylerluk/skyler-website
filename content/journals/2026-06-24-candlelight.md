I have not written by candlelight in quite some time. I remember those nights in Berkeley, Emeryville, where my comfort was in thoughts by candlelight. It's beautiful.

I remember the night in Korea, alone and weary. I remember nights back home, alone and satisfied. We need these things to ground us, when we have been far from home for too long. Writing by candlelight feels like coming home. I feel at peace here. It is good. And I am blessed.

This life is a beautiful thing. We live and we laugh and we live. And we remember. For we are human — destined to die, briefly ignited, but ultimately extinguished.

Skyler, though you may travel far, always remember your way back home. For it will ground you and keep you from losing your way.
